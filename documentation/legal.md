## LEGAL.md 

## Steps:

1. Repository & Licensing ArchitectureIsolate the assets: Ensure the README.md or main LICENSE file explicitly states that the contents of your /assets folder are excluded from the main open-source license.Dual-license disclaimer: Add a note explaining that while the code is open-source, the visual assets retain their original proprietary licenses.Check for raw distribution: Confirm that you are only distributing the icons as part of the functional application, not hosting them as a raw, downloadable "icon pack" for others to steal.

2. Flaticon Attribution PlacementIn-App credits: Verify that an "About", "Settings", or "Credits" screen inside your desktop app visibly displays: "Icons designed by [Author Name] from Flaticon".Public web page credits: Ensure the public website hosting your installer includes the Flaticon attribution links in the footer or credits section.GitHub Repository credits: Place the attribution text and a link back to Flaticon in your main repository README.md.

3. Branding & Trademark BoundariesApp Logo check: Confirm that you have not used a Flaticon icon as the main, trademarked logo/brand identity of your app. (Flaticon prohibits using their icons as registered or official logos).No ownership claims: Ensure nothing in your documentation implies that you or your project contributors created these graphics.

4. Installer & Distribution ComplianceBundled license text: Include this LEGAL.md or a copy of the Flaticon license terms inside the final installer package so it copies over to the user's desktop upon installation.
